Geekery



Have you ever tried to steal Boba Fett's blaster while Doctor Aphra judged you? No? Just me?




Kate has been a fangirl for most of her life. Her first true love is Star Wars; Kate's aunt played the VHS for A New Hope when she was five years old, and the rest is history. Kate's current favorite fandoms are Star Wars, The Witcher, and The Last of Us. Her interests span these franchise's video games, films, television shows, and written material.

Kate co-founded Book Wars Pod, a podcast exploring multimedia storytelling through the Star Wars franchise. The podcast covers the Star Wars books, video games, television shows, comics, and, of course, the films, with a focus on how these media intersect and inform one another to create a rich, multifacted fictional universe.

As a podcast co-host, Kate has moderated and participated in panels at fan conventions, including Washington, DC's Awesome Con and Star Wars Celebration Chicago. Panel topics have included diversity in Star Wars media, in terms of both characters and creators, as well as how Star Wars storytelling has operated since the formation of the Lucasfilm Story Group.

Kate is also a co-founder of #SWRepMatters, a movement that began as a social media hashtag campaign to raise awareness about what Star Wars does well in terms of diversity, and where it can improve. Each month, the #SWRepMatters planning group decides on a marginalized group to highlight in particular in a social media discussion that anyone can join. Past campaigns have focused on Black women, queer representation, body diversity, and the disabled community.

Kate has co-written for ElevenThirtyEight and is a staff writer for Tosche Station, the latter of whose podcast network Book Wars Pod is a member.
